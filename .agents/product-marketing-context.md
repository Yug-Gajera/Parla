# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: April 2026*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Parlova is an AI-powered language learning app that teaches you to actually *speak* — not just swipe flashcards.
**What it does:** Parlova immerses learners in realistic AI conversations, graded reading/listening content, and structured learning pathways — all calibrated to their CEFR level (A1–C2). It's built for people who want fluency, not streaks.
**Product category:** AI Language Learning Platform
**Product type:** SaaS (Web App)
**Business model:** Freemium — Free tier with weekly limits (3 conversations, 5 articles, 3 stories, 10 word lookups/day). Pro tier with unlimited access via Stripe subscription.
**Status:** Live and usable. Currently supports Spanish. Chinese, French, Japanese, Portuguese, and German are planned — Chinese is next because the founder needs it himself.

## Target Audience
**Primary audience:** Serious language learners (18–35) who are frustrated with gamified apps that don't build real speaking ability. Skews toward self-motivated adults — college students, remote workers, travel enthusiasts, career-driven professionals.
**Secondary audience:** Indie hackers and young builders who follow the journey of a solo founder building in public.
**Primary use case:** Learning to speak a new language confidently enough to have real conversations with native speakers.
**Jobs to be done:**
- "I want to practice speaking without the anxiety of talking to a real person first"
- "I want to learn vocabulary in context, not through random word lists"
- "I want a structured path from zero to conversational, not just games"
- "I want to consume real content (articles, stories, podcasts) at my level"
**Use cases:**
- College student preparing for study abroad
- Professional learning Spanish for a new market/role
- Anime fan wanting to watch without subtitles (Japanese)
- Traveler preparing for an extended trip
- Heritage speaker wanting to formalize their skills

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Mainstream language apps (Duolingo, Babbel, Busuu) gamify language learning so heavily that users complete lessons for months but can't hold a basic conversation. The gap between "I have a 300-day streak" and "I can order food in Spanish" is enormous.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Duolingo: Gamified dopamine loop, not fluency. Translating sentences ≠ speaking.
- Babbel: Better structure but still no real conversation practice.
- italki/Preply: Great for tutors, but expensive ($15-30/hr) and intimidating for beginners.
- YouTube/podcasts: No structure, no feedback, no progression.
**What it costs them:** Months or years of "learning" without being able to actually use the language. Money spent on apps and courses with nothing to show for it. The embarrassment of telling people "I'm learning Spanish" for 2 years and not being able to speak it.
**Emotional tension:** Frustration ("why can't I speak after all this time?"), self-doubt ("maybe I'm just bad at languages"), anxiety about speaking with real people.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Duolingo — gamified, massive user base, but doesn't teach real speaking. Users plateau at A2-B1.
**Direct:** Babbel — more structured, but still lesson-based without real conversation practice.
**Secondary:** italki/Preply — real tutors, but expensive and intimidating for beginners who aren't ready to speak with humans yet.
**Secondary:** ChatGPT/Claude for language practice — free and flexible, but no structure, no level tracking, no curriculum.
**Indirect:** YouTube polyglots, language learning subreddits, textbooks — fragmented, no feedback loop.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **AI conversation practice with real scenarios** — Not synthetic exercises. Ordering coffee, negotiating at a market, handling a doctor visit — with unpredictable twists that mirror real life.
- **Structured beginner pathway** — 3-phase Learn → Practice → Speak method that builds confidence before throwing you into conversation.
- **Full immersion content hub** — Articles, AI stories, books, podcasts, videos — all filtered by your actual CEFR level.
- **Spaced repetition integrated with reading** — Tap any word while reading to save it. SM-2 algorithm schedules reviews.
- **CEFR-aligned assessment** — AI diagnostic test to accurately place you, not a self-reported "I'm intermediate."

**How we do it differently:** Instead of isolated lessons, Parlova creates a full immersion environment. You learn vocabulary in a structured module, practice it in exercises, then use it in a real conversation — all in one flow. Between conversations, you read, listen, and watch content at your level to build passive skills.

**Why that's better:** Because that's how you actually learn a language — through exposure, practice, and real use. Not through translating "the cat is on the table" for 200 days.

**Why customers choose us:** "It's the first app where I actually practiced *speaking* from day one."

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I already use Duolingo" | Duolingo is great for building a habit. Parlova is for when you're ready to actually speak. Use both — Duo for streaks, Parlova for fluency. |
| "Can AI really teach me to speak?" | AI gives you unlimited, judgment-free speaking practice at any hour. It's not a replacement for human conversation — it's the bridge that gets you ready for it. |
| "There are only Spanish scenarios" | Starting focused = higher quality. More languages are coming, and the core learning method works for any language. |

**Anti-persona:** Someone who wants to casually learn 5 words a day with no pressure. Duolingo is perfect for them. Parlova is for people who actually want to get fluent.
